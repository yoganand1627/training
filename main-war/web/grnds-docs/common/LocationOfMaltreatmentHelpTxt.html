DFCS Homes:
2106.6 Reports of Abuse and Neglect in DFCS Homes

DFCS Homes are placement resources developed and approved by DFCS for the placement of children in the legal custody of DFCS/DHR. DFCS homes include the following placement types:

  • Foster homes, foster/adoptive homes, and adoptive homes prior to finalization. This includes therapeutic and specialized foster homes receiving level of care (LOC) payments.

  • Relative placements such as relative foster homes, relative foster/adoptive homes, relative adoptive homes prior to finalization, approved relative homes receiving TANF benefits, or approved relative free homes.

  • Granny Houses are approved resources for children of mothers with addiction problems generally located in public housing and staffed by elder adults.

  • Interstate Compact on the Placement of Children (ICPC) approved placements of children in the custody of another state, who are in an approved Georgia family foster home, foster/adoptive home, adoptive home, or relative placement.




    • Non DFCS Homes (CPA)
    2106.15 Reports of Abuse and Neglect in Private Agency and State Operated Homes

    The division is responsible for conducting investigations of abuse and neglect in private agency family foster homes; foster/adoptive homes, adoptive homes and state operated family foster homes regardless of the legal custody status of the children placed in these resources. Children in these settings may be in the custody of their parents, DFCS/DHR, the private agency; committed to the Department of Juvenile Justice (DJJ), or in joint custody of DFCS/DHR and DJJ. As reports are received, question reporters about the legal status of children in a private agency or state operated home. Reports that involve children in the legal custody of DFCS/DHR require notification to the legal county and an immediate to 24-hour response time. Private agency and state operated homes are defined as follows:

  • Private Agency Homes: Family foster homes, foster/adoptive homes, and adoptive homes developed and approved by private agencies licensed by the Office of Regulatory Services (ORS) for the placement of children. This includes therapeutic and specialized foster homes receiving LOC payments. Children placed in these homes may be in the custody of their parents, the private agency, or DFCS. The division purchases foster care services from DFCS approved private agencies for the placement of children in our legal custody. (See Foster Care Services Manual, Appendix H, for a listing of these agencies. The county department can verify the current approval status of an agency by contacting the Social Services Section, Provider Support Unit.) The State Adoption Unit contracts with licensed adoption agencies to provide foster/adoptive home and adoptive home placements and supervision for children who are in our temporary or permanent custody. Counties can verify the current approval status by contacting the State Adoption Unit. Some state agencies, MH/MR/SA and DJJ, also contract with private agencies for the placement of children.
  • State Operated Homes: Family foster home resources developed and approved by other state agencies such as MH/MR/SA, DJJ, and the agencies/associations with which they have contracts. MH/MR/SA provides therapeutic foster home resources for children. Children placed in MH/MR/SA resources may be in the legal custody of their parents, DFCS/DHR, or in the joint custody of DFCS/DHR and DJJ. DJJ has family contract homes for children adjudicated unruly or delinquent. Children placed in these resources may be in the legal custody of DFCS/DHR, in joint custody of DFCS/DHR and DJJ, or DJJ.




    • Residential Facilities
    2106.23 Reports of Abuse and Neglect in Residential Facilities

    Residential facilities are defined as child caring institutions or facilities that provide, either primarily or incidentally, full-time care for children under 18 years of age, outside of their own homes. Residential facilities generally care for more than six (6) children.

    Some examples of residential facilities are:
  • Private and state operated MH/MR/SA Child and Adolescent Group Homes
  • DJJ attention homes
  • Private group homes/child caring institutions
  • Private and county operated emergency shelters
  • Outdoor Therapeutic Programs (OTP)
  • Private and state operated hospitals, including hospitals providing 24-hour care and having the primary function of diagnosing and treating patients with psychiatric disorders
  • Private and state operated nursing homes
  • Private and state intensive and intermediate residential treatment facilities
  • Private and state residential schools and residential military schools
  • DJJ Regional Youth Detention Centers (RYDC) and Youth Detention Centers (YDC)
  • Department of Corrections boot camps
  • Residential camps




    • Schools
    2106.29 Reports of Abuse and Neglect in Public and Private Non-Residential Schools

    DFCS has the authority and responsibility for accepting reports of suspected abuse and neglect of children by school personnel in public and private non-residential schools. There is a fundamental difference between public and private schools in the State of Georgia:

  • Public non-residential schools are under the oversight of the Department of Education (DOE), but are administered by area, county, or independent boards of education.

  • Private non-residential schools are not licensed, regulated or certified by any public agency and do not have to meet the same state standards or laws governing public non-residential schools. Private non-residential school administrators set internal policies and discipline methods for private schools. When parents entrust the care of their child to a private non-residential school, they grant those administrators the same rights to discipline their child as the child's parent has.




    • Non Residential Facilities
    2106.34 Reports of Abuse or Neglect in Non-Residential Facilities

    DFCS has authority and responsibility for accepting reports and conducting investigations of suspected child abuse and neglect in non-residential facilities regardless of licensing status. Some examples of non-residential facilities included in this category are:

  • Family day care: A private residence operated by any person who receives pay for supervision and care fewer than 24 hours per day, without transfer of legal custody, of between three but no more than six children under 18 years of age who are not related to, nor live with the caregiver. Family day care providers are required to register with ORS.

  • Group day care: Any facility operated by any person, partnership, association, or corporation wherein are received for pay for group care not less than seven (7) nor more than eighteen (18) children under eighteen years of age for less than 24 hours without transfer of legal custody and which is required to be licensed or commissioned by the ORS.

  • Day care centers: Any facility operated by a person, society, agency, corporation, institution or group wherein are received for pay for group care, for fewer than 24 hours per day without transfer of legal custody, 19 or more children under 18 years of age, and which is required to be licensed or commissioned by ORS.

  • School Readiness Programs: The Office of School Readiness has licensure responsibility for children enrolled in Child Care Centers which are approved to operate Georgia Pre-K programs.

  • Head Start Programs: Head Start Programs have three categories of licensure: Head Start Programs that receive funding through the Georgia Pre-K Program are licensed through the Office of School Readiness. Head Start Programs that receive no funding through the Georgia Pre-K Program are licensed through ORS. Some Head Start Programs remain unlicensed.

  • Public and private day camps and recreational programs

  • MH/MR/SA day programs: the MH/MR/SA Regional Boards supervise these programs. A designee from these regional boards will participate in the joint investigation.
