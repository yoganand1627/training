Provisions of Agreement


This agreement for an adoption subsidy, which is signed prior to the effective date and finalization of the adoption, is entered into by and between the "Department" and the adoptive parent(s) for the purposes of facilitating the legal adoption of the above named child. The adoptive parent(s) verify that they will be unable to adopt said child without a financial and/or medical subsidy. The adoptive parent(s) agree that he/she/they intend to adopt the child [[txtChildFirstName]] and agree(s) to the terms and provisions contained in this document.

The adoptive parent(s) agree that he/she/they intend to adopt the child named above and have signed this document prior to finalization of the adoption for the purpose of receiving Adoption Assistance payments and/or services for the child under Title-IV-E (Federal/State funded), Title IV-B (State funded) Adoption Assistance, Non-Recurring Adoption Expenses, and/or Medicaid from the time of adoptive placement.

  1. General Provisions

    Following the adoption finalization:

    1. This agreement may not be amended, suspended, or terminated, except as outlined in this agreement (Sections II.B., V., VI., VII.) or by mutual written agreement of the parties.
    2. This agreement cannot be transferred by the adoptive parent(s) to any other party. However, in the event of the death of the adoptive parent(s) or termination of their parental rights, the child may remain eligible for assistance in a subsequent adoption. The new subsidy agreement must be approved by the Department prior to the finalization of the adoption.

      • Please note the successor adoptive parent(s) must contact the County Department.

    3. An ongoing monthly payment can be issued only to the adoptive parent. In the event that there is a change in the custodial status of the child, the Department must be notified within 30 days. If a change in payee is necessary (i.e. divorce or death of an adoptive parent), a written request must be sent to the County Adoption Assistance worker with the supporting legal documentation attached. The County Department will consult with the Social Services Administration Unit in order to make a decision that is in line with Federal and State policy.

  2. Assistance

    1. Non-recurring Adoption Assistance (UAS 510) (State Funds Only):

      The Department agrees to reimburse the adoptive parent(s) for expenses that are reasonable and necessary for the adoption to occur, up to [[NON_RECURRING_LIMIT_AMT]] per child for children deemed to meet the Special Needs Criteria. Non-recurring Adoption Assistance is not approved when Adoption Assistance is Deferred.

      In order for Non-recurring Adoption Assistance to be paid, the expenses must:

      1. Directly relate to the legal adoption, and,
        • Legal /Court fees
        • Travel/Lodging/Food for pre-placement visits.
        • Physicals for adoptive parent(s)' adoptive home evaluation

      2. Not be in violation of state of federal law; and,

      3. Not have been reimbursed for other sources of funds

      Reimbursement may only be applied for after adoption finalization. Reimbursement can only occur within 2 years of finalization.

    2. Monthly Cash Payment:

      1. The amount of the monthly Adoption Assistance cash payment is based on the needs of the child and the circumstances of the adoptive parent(s). The amount is determined by mutual agreement between the adoptive parent(s) and the Department.

      2. The amount of the Adoption Assistance payment may not exceed the Department's Family Foster Care Board Rate or Specialized DFCS Family Foster Care Board Rate the child would have received in Family Foster Care immediately prior to the adoptive placement.

      3. If the monthly Adoption Assistance payment designated on this Agreement exceeds the assigned Department (DFCS) Family Foster Care Board Rate or Specialized Family Foster Care Board Rate, the monthly payment will be adjusted to an amount that does not exceed the assigned Family Foster Care Board Rate or Specialized Family Foster Care Board Rate. The adoptive parent(s) shall be given written notice of any adjustments to their monthly payment made pursuant to this paragraph. The adjustment will take effect in the month immediately following the month the adoptive parents receive notice of the adjustment.

      4. Adjustments in Adoption Assistance payments which decrease the monthly payment amount may only be made through mutual agreement with the adoptive parent(s) based upon changes in the needs of the child or family circumstances. An exception to this requirement is the case in which across-the-board statewide adjustments are made which lower the monthly payment rate. Adjustments in Adoption Assistance payments which increase the monthly payment amount will not be made after the signing of the Adoption Assistance Agreement, unless a child has a documented physical, mental, or emotional disability, as certified by a licensed physician or psychologist and a waiver packet is approved by the Social Services Administration Unit. Any rate increase still may not exceed what the child received while in foster care. An exception to this rule would be across-the-board statewide increases in the monthly payment authorized by the Department due to already-designated age level increases.

      5. If it is determined by the Department that an overpayment has been made to the adoptive parent(s), the Department shall have the authority to collect the overpayment through a mutual agreement with the adoptive parent(s). If this results in unsuccessful collection, the Department shall have the authority to pursue other legal remedies.

      6. If the child in this Agreement is placed in Family Foster Care or a residential facility at state expense, the adoptive parent(s) may continue to receive Adoption Assistance payments if the permanency plan is reunification and the family's ongoing legal and financial responsibility for the child can be established. For a child placed in a residential facility, the adoptive parent(s) will be asked to contribute to the child's out of home expenses. If the State of Georgia determines that the adopted parent(s) are no longer legally or financially responsible for the child, Adoption Assistance will be terminated.

      7. If the State of Georgia determines the adoptive parent(s) are no longer legally or financially responsible for the child(ren) and continues to receive Adoption Assistance payments, the family will be responsible for reimbursing the overpayment to the county department. If this results in unsuccessful collection, the Department shall have the authority to pursue other legal remedies.

    4. Deferred Adoption Assistance

      Deferred Adoption Assistance may be available for children who:

      1. Were in the permanent custody of the Department (both parents' rights terminated) at the time of adoptive placement (signing of Agreements) OR were placed from the Department's temporary custody into the permanent custody of a specified relative or individual for the purpose of adoption at a Department-initiated TPR hearing.

      2. Do not currently exhibit any documented physical, mental, or emotional disability, as certified by a licensed physician or psychologist. Such conditions may be evidenced by the parent's medical history (e.g., drug involvement, mental illness or HIV status), genetic predisposition or child's background (serious abuse or neglect whose full effects have not yet been realized).

        The amount of the Adoption Assistance payment may not exceed the Department's Family Foster Care Board Rate the child would have received in Family Foster Care immediately prior to the adoptive placement.

        NOTE: Any future request for a special needs determination to activate a Deferred Application must be related to a documented physical, mental, or emotional disability, as certified by a licensed physician or psychologist. (Payments shall commence the month the determination of special needs is made by the Department / Social Services Administration Unit)

    5. Social Services

      While this agreement remains in effect;

      1. Social Services are provided under Title XX of the Social Security Act will be provided to this child in accordance with the procedures of the state in which he/she resides.

      2. Title XX services are available to Title IV-E Adoption Assistance recipients in their state of residence.

    6. Out of State Relocation

      When moving to another State the family must notify the [[txtCountyName2]] County Department's office of their pending move. They must advise of the intended date of relocation, the new physical and mailing address and a phone number. Monthly adoption assistance payments will continue to be provided by [[txtCountyName3]] County Department's Office. For Title IV-E (Federal/State funded) eligible children, medical coverage will continue to be provided per the procedures of the Interstate Compact on Adoption and Medical Assistance (ICAMA). For Title IV-B (State funded only) eligible children, the State of Georgia is not responsible for medical coverage when the adoptive family and child move to another state.

    7. Other

      When the adoptive parent(s) receive SSI payments for this child, it shall be the Department's responsibility to inform the Social Security Administration in writing if the child is to receive adoption assistance payments. The adoptive parents of children receiving SSI are responsible for notifying the Social Security Administration of the finalization of the adoption, change of payee, and receipt of adoption assistance benefits.

  3. Post-Adoption Services

    The Adoptive parent(s) and the Department agree that post-adoption services may be provided in accordance with the availability of state funds, services and resources in the Department and community. It is understood that post adoption services is not a continuation of supervision but an agency service given as needed and requested by any of the parties involved in the adoption, when in accordance with eligibility guidelines established by the Department. After the finalization of an adoption, post-adoption services to eligible families may include, but are not limited to:

    1. Information, Referral, and Support

    2. Crisis Intervention

    3. Reunion Registry Assistance

    4. Respite for Medically Fragile Children, as described in Section 109 of the Adoption Services Policy Manual.

  4. Notification of Change

    1. The Adoptive parent(s) shall within ten (10) business days notify the Department, in writing, of any of the following events:

      1. Change of address

      2. The child's death, marriage or entry into the military service

      3. The child is no longer attending school on a full time basis (not GED or Job Corps) after age 18 (If child meets eligibility criteria to receive monthly adoption assistance past age 18).

      4. If the adoptive parent(s) are no longer legally or financially responsible for the support of the child.

      5. Any report of substantiated child abuse or neglect or the temporary removal of the child from the home.

      6. The child no longer resides in the home.

  5. Termination

    Termination of the Adoption Assistance Agreement will occur in any of the following circumstances upon thirty (30) days written notice to the adoptive parent(s), by certified mail. Termination must be based on verified information.

    1. This agreement expires the last day of the child's 18th birth month (no exceptions)

    2. The adoptive parent(s) request that the payments permanently stop.

    3. The adoptive parent(s) is no longer legally (parental rights legally terminated) or financially responsible for the child.

    4. The child marries or is emancipated by other means.

    5. The child enlists in the military.

    6. The Adoptive parent(s) die.

    7. The child dies.

    8. The child turns age 18 but does not meet the Basic Criterion and Requirements to receive Adoption Assistance benefits past age 18.

  6. Duration of the Agreement

    This Adoption Assistance Agreement will terminate at the end of the above-named child's 18th birth month, unless termination occurs earlier as the result of one or more of the conditions set forth in Section V. "Termination".

    If the Department / Social Services Administration Unit determines that a child is eligible for State Funded IV-B Adoption Assistance benefits beyond age 18, a new Adoption Assistance Agreement for children over age 18 must be fully signed and executed prior to the child's 18th birthday.

    Please Note: Non DHS Private/Independent Adoptions do not qualify for Adoption Assistance beyond the child's 18th birth month.

    A child may be eligible to receive State-funded IV-B Adoption Assistance (Program 508) beginning the month after the child's 18th birthday, but this eligibility is contingent upon the availability of State funds and the child meeting defined criteria and requirements through high school and/or college (see below).

    Eligibility through High School Only:

    Criteria:

    Only children who were placed on adoptive status while in the permanent custody of DHS are eligible for benefits beyond age 18, or those children who were placed from DHS temporary custody into the permanent custody of a specified relative or individual for the purpose of adoption as a disposition of a DHS-initiated TPR hearing.

    Requirement: Quarterly verification from School verifying full-time attendance

    Eligibility through College/Technical School:

    Criteria:

    Only children who were placed on adoptive status while in the permanent custody of DHS are eligible for benefits beyond age 18, or those children who were placed from DHS temporary custody into the permanent custody of a specified relative or individual for the purpose of adoption as a disposition of a DHS-initiated TPR hearing and the child must meet one of the two criteria below:

    Placed on adoption status after age 13, or
    Placed on adoptive status prior to July 1, 1998

    Requirement: Quarterly verification from College/Technical School verifying full-time enrollment/attendance (12 hours per term)

    Information regarding these criteria and requirements must be discussed with the County Department and can be viewed online at: http://www.odis.dhr.state.ga.us/3000_fam/3120_ado/Manual/109.doc

  7. Amendments

    This agreement may be amended or cancelled at any time by mutual agreement of the Department and the adoptive parent(s). Such amendment or cancellation shall be in writing as provided in Department rules and no oral modifications made by any employee or agent of the Department or any party to this agreement shall have any effect.

  8. Appeal

    Adoptive parent(s) may appeal the Department's decision to deny, reduce, change or terminate Monthly Adoption Assistance or Non-recurring Adoption Assistance benefits in accordance with the rules and procedures of the state's fair hearing and appeal process, except where the change was made pursuant to paragraph II (B)(4-5).

    Application for appeal should be made to the County Department within 10 business days of denial of Monthly Adoption Assistance or Non-recurring benefits.

In addition, I am aware that if I make a willfully false statement or representation, or use other fraudulent methods to obtain assistance to which I am not entitled or greater than that to which I am entitled, I can be found guilty of a felony or misdemeanor under appropriate state or federal law.
