PERSON HOME STUDY
The following outline contains information to be included in the home study. Workers may choose to follow the home study guide in Appendix I/7330.
 
1. Identifying data on caseworker performing the homestudy:
 
  a. Name of worker performing study.
[[txtNameofWorkerPerformingStudy]]
 
  b. Address of worker.
[[txtAddressofWorker]]
 
  c. Date study is prepared.
[[txtDateStudyPrepared]]
 
2. Summary of Contacts:
 
  a. Number of visits and interviews with the family. (List dates and location of contacts.)
[[txtSummaryofContacts]]
 
  b. Family's experience with other agencies.
[[txtFamilysExperienceOtherAgencies]]
 
3. Identifying data on each adult member of the family with whom child may be placed:
 
  a. Name and how related to child to be placed.
[[txtNameandHowRelatedtoChild]]
 
  b. Age 
[[txtAgeofAdultMemberofFamily]]
 
  c. Sex 
[[txtSexofAdultMemberofFamily]]
 
4. Description of physical appearance and personality of adult family members:
 
  a. Special aspects of physical appearance.
[[txtSpecialAspectsofPhysicalAppearanceofAdultFamilyMember]]
 
  b. Brief description of personality.
[[txtBriefDescriptionofPersonalityofAdultFamilyMember]]
 
  c. Health and health history.
[[txtHealthandHealthHistoryofAdultFamilyMember]]
 
  d. Religion
[[txtReligionofAdultFamilyMember]]
 
  e. Occupation
[[txtOccupationofAdultFamilyMember]]
 
  f. Feelings about placement of child(ren) in their home.
[[txtFeelingsaboutPlacementofChildreninHome]]
 
5. Quality of marriage
 
  a. Verification of marriage
[[txtVerificationofMarriage]]
 
  b. Previous marriages and divorces, dates
[[txtPreviousMarriageandDivorcesandDates]]
 
  c. Evaluation of previous marriages
[[txtEvaluationofPreviousMarriages]]
 
  d. Roles of husband and wife and relationship to each other.
[[txtRolesofHusbandandWifeandRelationship]]
 
6. Background and inter-personal relationships
 
  a. Family of origin; how disciplined; feelings about it.
[[txtFamilyOfOriginDesciplined]]
 
  b. Present relationships with extended family and friends.
[[txtPresentRelationshipswithExtendedFamily]]
 
  c. Ability to handle stress, including problems arising from the placement.
[[txtAbilitytoHandleStress]]
 
7. Children living in the home:
 
  a. Name. 
[[txtNameofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  b. Age. 
[[txtAgeofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  c. Sex. 
[[txtSexofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  d. Grade. 
[[txtGradeofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  e. Feelings about placement of other children.
[[txtFeelingsAboutPlacementfromChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  f. Brief description of personalities.
[[txtBriefDescriptionofPersonalitiesofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
  g. Health Issues.
[[txtHealthIssuesofChildrenlivinginHome]]
 
8. Other Children cared for in the home:
 
  a. Name. 
[[txtNameofOtherChildreninHome]]
 
  b. Age. 
[[txtAgeofOtherChildreninHome]]
 
  c. Sex. 
[[txtSexofOtherChildreninHome]]
 
  d. General behaviors and special problems.
[[txtGeneralBehaviorsofOtherChildreninHome]]
 
9. Living arrangements:
 
  a. Physical description of the house.
[[txtPhysicalDescriptionofHouse]]
 
  b. Sleeping arrangements.
[[txtSleepingArrangements]]
 
  c. Safety issues (if any).
[[txtSafetyIssues]]
 
  d. Proximity to child's parents.
[[txtProximitytoChildsParents]]
 
10. Financial
 
  a. Sources of family income, working hours, total family monthly expenses.
[[txtSourcesofFamilyIncome]]
 
  b. Willingness and ability to support the child.
[[txtWillingnessandAbilitytoSupportChild]]
 
  c. Means of verification of employment, hours, length of employment and documentation of pay.
[[txtMeansofVerificationofEmployment]]
 
11. Family's expectations:
 
  a. How realistic is the family about problems the child may have and how they will deal with them.
[[txtHowRealisticisFamiliyaboutProblems]]
 
  b. Are they willing to protect the child from parents or relatives who may pressure them to let them have the child.
[[txtAretheyWillingtoProtectChildfromParents]]
 
  c. What are their reasons for wanting the child.
[[txtReasonsforWantingChild]]
 
  d. Do they want the child temporarily or permanently and how does this compare with the requesting agency's desires.
[[txtChildTemporaryorPermanently]]
 
  e. How well does the family know the child? Has there been previous involvement?
[[txtHowDoesFamilyknowChild]]
 
  f. What is their understanding of why the child was removed?
[[txtTheirUnderstandingofWhyChildRemoved]]
 
  g. What are their attitudes toward the child's parents and other members of the child's family.
[[txtTheirAttitudestowardsChildsParents]]
 
  h. What are their attitudes towards parental visitation and contacts.
[[txtTheirAttitudestowardsParentalVisitation]]
 
  i. Are they willing to cooperate with visitation and other case plans and help siblings maintain contact.
[[txtWillingtoCooperatewithVisitation]]
 
12. Child rearing and discipline. Past experience and current plans:
 
  a. Family's plans for child's supervision and their appropriateness.
[[txtFamilysplansforChildsSupervision]]
 
  b. Form of discipline that will be used and who administers discipline
[[txtFormofDisciplineandWhoAdministers]]
 
  c. Past experience in child rearing (positive and negative).
[[txtPastExperienceinChildRearing]]
 
13. Reference:
 
Contact at least three references who have interacted with the family on an informal basis. At least one of these should be related to the family. Include the following information about each reference.
 
  a. Name, address, telephone number and relation to the child and the relative
[[txtNameAddressTelephoneNumberandRelationtoChild]]
 
  b. Comments about the family's abilities as parents and general character.
[[txtCommentsAboutFamiliesAbilitiesasParents]]
 
  c. The results of persons check and local CPS records.
[[txtResultsofPersonsCheckandLocalCPSRecords]]
 
  d. The results of the criminal background check.
[[txtResultsofCriminalBackgroundCheck]]
 
14. Evaluation
 
  a. Summarize the pros and cons of the prospective placement. Note any points of particular concern. If the placement would require support services, identify the services needed.
[[txtSummerizeProsandConsofPlacement]]
 
15. Recommendations
 
Choose one of the following alternatives:
 
  a. Recommend the placement.
[[txtRecommendthePlacement]]
 
  b. Recommend the placement on the condition that support services are provided.
[[txtRecommendthePlacementonCondition]]
 
  c. Defer a recommendation until additional information is available.
[[txtDeferRecommendation]]
 
  d. Recommend that the child be placed elsewhere.
[[txtRecommendChildPlacedElsewhere]]
